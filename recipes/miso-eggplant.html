Miso Eggplant

Description

This miso-roasted Japanese eggplant is basted in a gingery miso glaze that packs a punch of flavor. The best part of cooking with Japanese eggplant is that the skin gets soft and there is no need to peel it. If you're not a fan of cilantro, garnish with scallions instead.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon white miso paste
  • 2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 2 (4 ounce) Japanese eggplant, stems removed and halved lengthwise
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon chopped cilantro

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C), and line a baking sheet with aluminum foil.
  2. Whisk miso paste, sesame oil, rice vinegar, garlic, and ginger together in a small bowl until smooth.
  3. Place the halved eggplant on the baking sheet. Brush miso mixture over the eggplant.
  4. Bake eggplant in the preheated oven until tender and browned, abouTransfer to a serving dish and garnish with sesame seeds and cilantro.
